About

Where accounting
expertise meets AI.

I started my career in tax accounting at Rowshan & Co, doing full-cycle bookkeeping for 30+ small-business clients and spending hundreds of hours each tax season processing 1099s and W-2s by hand. That repetitive, error-prone work planted a question: what if AI could handle this?

Pursuing my M.S. in Business Analytics at Baruch College, I went deep into Python, machine learning, and generative AI — not as abstract concepts, but as tools to solve the exact problems I'd lived through. The result is a self-directed portfolio of accounting applications: I own the design, the engineering, and the delivery, with high-level direction and career guidance from mentors in AI and engineering.

My edge isn't just knowing Python or AI. It's understanding why a TIN mismatch on a 1099-NEC matters, what auditors actually look for in GL transactions, and how vendor payments map to tax schedules. Domain expertise + AI engineering — that's the combination.

"Accountants who can code are common. Accountants who've designed, built, and deployed AI applications that automate real accounting workflows — that's a different conversation entirely."

Project 01 · Main app

PREPARE

Deployed · Live

A reconciliation prep engine. Turns messy bank and credit card statement PDFs into review-ready 1099 pre-reconciliation workbooks — the workbook a CPA reviews before filing. Not a filer.

  • Row-level transaction classification via Claude PDF Skill (Agent SDK)
  • Two orthogonal integrity checks — statement math vs. extraction completeness
  • "Transcribe, don't compute" — AI reads & labels, deterministic logic does the math
  • Per-statement, consolidated, and audit-ready Excel surfaces

Project 02 · Main app

CASSIA

Deployed · Live · No-login

A chat-based accounting support workspace for small-business follow-up work. Ask in plain English; it routes to your books, the tax docs, or your saved work — and remembers findings by meaning. Formerly CoReckoner.

  • Hybrid router — Text-to-SQL, RAG, both, or semantic recall
  • Grounded answers with citations, tables, and auto-generated charts
  • Per-session document Q&A, saved-answer recall, topic organization
  • Deployed no-login; the full account system is built and frozen, re-enablable

Project 03 · Main app

LUCENT

Built · Deploying next

A pre-audit review packet for general-ledger exports. Narrows a large transaction population into a prioritized review queue, explains each risk indicator in plain language, and shows what evidence to request — risk indication, not fraud detection. (rebranded from ARGUS)

  • ML finds — Isolation Forest, 200 trees, label-free
  • Audit logic adjusts — materiality filter + PCAOB AS 2401 qualitative override
  • GPT explains — a validated 7-field memo with deterministic fallback
  • Anchored to AU-C 315, PCAOB AS 2401/2201, and COSO 2013

The arc continues

Two more apps, then the hub.

App 04 · Self-directed · In design

IRS Form Processor

A guided 1099/W-9 preparation and review workspace: collect vendor identity, detect reportable payment candidates from the books, validate TIN and missing-field issues, and export review-ready workpapers for accountant approval. Preparation and review — not e-filing.

App 05 · Self-directed · In design

Tax Schedule Classifier

Reviews vendor, GL, and P&L detail and proposes likely Schedule C/E/F line-item candidates with reasoning, confidence, and review flags for tax-preparer approval. It proposes candidates — it doesn't prepare or file a return.

The hub · Convergence

TAU

Transaction Agent Ultimate — where the portfolio began as a prototype, and where it ends. Each shipped app is compressed into a compact add-on, so the whole suite lives in one place. When all five fold in, the mission is complete.
